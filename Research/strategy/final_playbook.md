**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2026-06-14  
**Basis:** Cross-expert synthesis from 11 practitioner profiles (Matt Diggity, Brian Dean, Nathan Gotch, Koray GÜBÜR, Gael Breton, Julian Goldie, Lily Ray, Mark Williams-Cook, Ross Simmonds, Aleyda Solis) and the consolidated findings report.

## 1. Executive Summary

AI has permanently changed SEO content production—but not in the way most teams assume. The consensus across practitioners, from semantic SEO architects to B2B GEO strategists and agency automation operators, is unambiguous: **AI dramatically accelerates research, planning, and drafting; it does not replace editorial judgment, topical architecture, or trust signals.**

The winning model is a **human-governed production system** where:

- AI handles repeatable labor (research, briefs, first drafts, gap analysis, repurposing).
- Humans own strategy, differentiation, accuracy, and final approval.
- Content is architected topically—not published as disconnected articles.
- Pages are structured for both Google retrieval and LLM citation.
- Distribution and third-party corroboration are treated as equal priorities to on-site publishing.
- Measurement extends beyond rankings and clicks to AI presence, readiness, and business impact.

Experts disagree mainly on **automation depth**, **volume tolerance**, and **semantic complexity**—not on whether AI should be used or whether human review is optional. Even the most automation-forward operators (Julian Goldie, Gael Breton) enforce editorial gates; even the most technical architects (Koray GÜBÜR) insist on human editorial judgment for non-commodity insight.

**This playbook translates that consensus into an operational system your team can run in 90 days.**

### Core Operating Principles

| Principle | What It Means in Practice |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Cyborg, not autopilot | AI drafts; humans validate, differentiate, and approve |
| Architecture before volume | Map intent and topical structure before scaling production |
| Intent over keywords | Keywords are inputs; SERP format, prompts, and PAA chains are decision layers |
| Trust is non-fabricatable | Inject proprietary data, case studies, named experts, and firsthand experience |
| Owned quality → distribution → surface tactics | Build retrievable pages first; then earn citations off-site |
| Systems, not sessions | Persistent AI context, documented workflows, and defined quality gates |

## 3. Content Research Workflow

Research is the highest-leverage phase. Experts agree: **plan intent and architecture before generating content at scale.** Skipping research produces high-volume, semantically disconnected pages that increase crawl cost without building authority.

### Step 1: Confirm Brand Scope

Before researching topics, validate that each candidate connects to:

- Genuine business expertise
- A real product or service offering
- Commercial relevance (not generic filler for traffic alone)

**Gate:** If a topic cannot pass the "Is it on brand?" test (Aleyda Solis), remove it from the queue.

### Step 2: Intent and SERP Validation

For each candidate topic:

1. **Analyze SERP intent** — What format dominates? Informational guide, comparison, tool roundup, local result? (Brian Dean, Nathan Gotch, Matt Diggity)
2. **Identify the complete user problem** — What must the content fully solve, not partially address? (Brian Dean)
3. **Extract entities from top-ranking pages** — People, products, concepts, attributes competitors cover (Matt Diggity, Koray GÜBÜR)
4. **Map PAA follow-up chains** — Anticipate conversational query sequences (Mark Williams-Cook)

**Tools:** Ahrefs, SEMrush, AlsoAsked, SurferSEO (entity extraction), manual SERP review.

### Step 3: Build Topical Architecture

Organize research output into connected ecosystems:

| Level | Definition | Minimum Standard |
|-------|------------|------------------|
| Pillar page | Comprehensive hub for a root topic | 1 per core topic area |
| Cluster articles | Supporting pages covering subtopics, FAQs, comparisons | 4+ per pillar |
| Internal links | Contextual connections within silo | Every cluster links to pillar and related clusters |

**Advanced option (Koray GÜBÜR):** Build Semantic Content Networks covering all contextual domains a query fan-out might decompose into—not just the head term.

### Step 4: Generate and Filter Prompt Libraries

Move beyond keyword lists to ICP-driven prompt research:

1. Define ideal customer profiles with constraints: industry, team size, budget, geography, compliance needs.
2. Use AI to generate realistic conversational prompts from ICPs + seed keywords (Mark Williams-Cook).
3. Expand via PAA proximity mapping (AlsoAsked).
4. **Filter by grounding probability** — Prioritize queries where AI systems will retrieve from the web (Mark Williams-Cook):
   - Inspect ChatGPT `search_prob` (threshold ~0.65)
   - Use Gemini grounding API (~$0.03/query)
   - Apply ML classification tools for bulk filtering
5. Identify 3–4 background web searches AI performs for each grounded prompt.

**Output:** Prioritized production queue ranked by influence potential, not search volume alone.

### Step 5: Competitive and Citation Gap Analysis

- Run citation audits: where are competitors cited in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini? (Ross Simmonds)
- Identify formats that surface: comparative listicles, opinionated posts, ungated PDFs, Q&A pages.
- Map gaps: prompts where your brand is absent, misrepresented, or not recommended.

**Automate with:** Claude Code skills, n8n workflows, or MCP-connected research agents (Gael Breton, Julian Goldie).

### Research Workflow Diagram

```
Brand Scope Filter
       ↓
SERP Intent + Entity Extraction
       ↓
Topical Map (Pillar + Clusters)
       ↓
ICP Prompt Library → Grounding Filter
       ↓
Citation Gap Analysis
       ↓
Prioritized Content Brief Queue
```

### Research Output Template (Per Article)

Each brief should include:

- Target query / primary prompt
- SERP intent type and required format
- Secondary prompts and PAA follow-ups
- Entity coverage list
- Competitor URLs to beat
- Differentiation requirements (proprietary data, expert quote, case study)
- Internal linking plan (pillar, related clusters)
- Schema types to apply
- Distribution channels planned

## 4. Content Creation Workflow

The cyborg workflow is the consensus production model. AI generates; humans differentiate.

### Phase A: AI-Assisted Scaffolding

**Owner:** Content strategist or AI operator  
**Tools:** Claude/GPT with persistent context (CLAUDE.md), structured SEO prompts

1. Load persistent context: brand voice, ICP, product knowledge, competitor positioning.
2. Feed approved content brief.
3. AI generates:
   - Detailed outline aligned to SERP intent
   - First draft with keyword/query placement
   - FAQ block and Q&A subheadings
   - Meta title and description options
   - Internal linking suggestions

**Prompt requirements (Julian Goldie, Matt Diggity):**

- Target query and intent type
- Required tone and audience
- Competitor analysis summary
- Entity list to cover naturally
- CTA format and placement rules
- Prohibitions: no fabricated stats, no generic filler, no unsourced claims

### Phase B: Human Differentiation Layer

**Owner:** Senior editor or subject matter expert  
**Non-negotiable:** Every draft must pass human rewrite—not just proofreading.

Editors must add:

- [ ] Original insight, opinion, or framework not present in AI output
- [ ] Proprietary data, case studies, or firsthand experience
- [ ] Named expert attribution or verifiable citations
- [ ] Brand voice alignment
- [ ] Fact-checked claims (Lily Ray)
- [ ] Justifiable claims with specific numbers, dates, and sources (Ross Simmonds)

**Rule:** If the final article could have been written by any competitor's AI, it fails differentiation.

### Phase C: On-Page and Entity Optimization

**Owner:** Technical SEO reviewer or content optimizer

| Element | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| H1 | Primary query/prompt |
| H2–H3 | Subtopics phrased as real questions where appropriate |
| Opening | Direct answer to core query in first 1–2 paragraphs |
| Entities | Woven naturally in headings, body, alt text, anchor text |
| Schema | FAQ, How-To, Article, author markup as applicable |
| Internal links | Contextual links to pillar and related clusters |
| Media | Original or licensed images with descriptive alt text |
| Structure | Standalone answer paragraphs extractable by LLMs |

**Advanced (Koray GÜBÜR):** Apply Relevance Configuration—align phrasing with how retrieval systems tokenize language. Treat visual layout as a signal; avoid identical templated designs across all pages.

### Phase D: Readiness Pre-Check

Before entering review queue, verify against Aleyda Solis readiness characteristics (detailed in Section 5):

Accessible · Extractable · Useful · Fresh · Differentiated · Recognizable · Consistent · Corroborated · Credible · Transactable

### Production Velocity Controls

| Site Age | Daily Publish Cap | Rationale |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| < 12 months | 3–5 quality posts max | Avoid velocity signals on new domains (Matt Diggity) |
| Established (12+ months) | Scale with audit cadence | Monthly merge/expand audits required |
| Any site | No raw AI publishing | Universal expert consensus |

## 5. Human Review Workflow

Human-in-the-loop is non-negotiable. Every profile that addresses production workflow rejects publishing raw AI output.

### Three-Gate Review System

#### Gate 1: Editorial Review (Content Quality)

**Reviewer:** Senior editor or SME  
**Focus:** Accuracy, voice, differentiation, completeness

| Check | Pass Criteria |
|-------|---------------|
| Fact accuracy | All claims verified; no hallucinated stats or citations (Lily Ray) |
| Completeness | Solves the full user problem, not a partial answer (Brian Dean) |
| Differentiation | Contains non-replicable value: data, experience, expert input |
| Voice | Matches brand tone; reads as human-authored |
| E-E-A-T | Author bio present; expertise demonstrated; trust signals visible |
| Originality | Passes internal "commodity test"—not interchangeable with competitor AI content |

**Outcome:** Approved → Gate 2 | Rejected → Return to editor with specific rewrite notes

#### Gate 2: Technical SEO Review (Retrieval Optimization)

**Reviewer:** SEO specialist  
**Focus:** Structure, entities, schema, links, extractability

| Check | Pass Criteria |
|-------|---------------|
| Intent match | Format matches SERP/prompt intent type |
| Heading structure | Query in H1; logical H2–H3 hierarchy |
| Entity coverage | Required entities present naturally |
| Schema markup | Valid FAQ/How-To/Article/author JSON-LD |
| Internal linking | Links to pillar + related clusters in context |
| LLM extractability | Standalone answer paragraphs; Q&A blocks |
| Page speed / HTML | Clean, fast, accessible markup |
| Readiness score | Passes 10 readiness characteristics (below) |

**Outcome:** Approved → Gate 3 | Rejected → Return with technical fix list

#### Gate 3: Brand and Legal Review (Governance)

**Reviewer:** Brand manager or legal (as required)  
**Focus:** Brand alignment, compliance, commercial accuracy

| Check | Pass Criteria |
|-------|---------------|
| Brand scope | Topic connects to real expertise and offerings (Aleyda Solis) |
| Claims | No unsubstantiated superlatives or regulatory risk |
| CTAs | Accurate product/service representation |
| Disclosure | AI assistance disclosed if required by policy |
| Entity consistency | Brand name, product names, and positioning match across properties |

**Outcome:** Approved → Publishing queue | Rejected → Return with governance notes

### Readiness Checklist (Pre-Publish)

Use Aleyda Solis's ten structural characteristics as the final quality gate:

| Characteristic | Question to Ask |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **Accessible** | Is the page indexable, fast, and mobile-friendly? |
| **Extractable** | Can an LLM pull standalone answers from clear paragraphs and headings? |
| **Useful** | Does it genuinely help the user, per Helpful Content standards? |
| **Fresh** | Is the information current with dated references where needed? |
| **Differentiated** | Does it offer something competitors and AI cannot easily replicate? |
| **Recognizable** | Are brand, author, and entity signals clear? |
| **Consistent** | Do entity signals match site, LinkedIn, Wikidata, and reviews? |
| **Corroborated** | Is the brand/topic supported by off-site mentions and citations? |
| **Credible** | Are claims sourced, experts named, and E-E-A-T visible? |
| **Transactable** | Does it connect to a clear next step aligned with business goals? |

**Minimum passing score:** All ten characteristics must pass or have a documented remediation plan before publish.

## 6. Publishing Workflow

Publishing is not the end of the workflow—it is the handoff point to distribution and measurement.

### Pre-Publish Final Checklist

- [ ] All three review gates passed with sign-off documented
- [ ] Readiness checklist completed (10/10 or remediated)
- [ ] Schema validated (Google Rich Results Test or equivalent)
- [ ] Internal links live and contextual
- [ ] Author page and bio linked
- [ ] Meta title, description, and OG tags set
- [ ] URL slug optimized and consistent with silo structure
- [ ] Images compressed with descriptive alt text
- [ ] Publish date and last-updated date set
- [ ] Analytics and AI tracking tags in place

### Publish Sequence

Follow Ross Simmonds's Prioritized GEO pyramid—owned authoritative content first:

1. **Publish on owned domain** — Build indexable, retrievable pages that RAG systems can fetch.
2. **Submit to index** — Sitemap updated; Search Console inspection if needed.
3. **Activate distribution queue** — Trigger repurposing and off-site placement workflows (Section 7).
4. **Log in content registry** — Track URL, target prompts, pillar/cluster relationship, publish date, reviewer sign-offs.

### Post-Publish Actions (Within 48 Hours)

- [ ] Verify indexing status
- [ ] Share to owned social channels (LinkedIn, YouTube community, newsletter)
- [ ] Queue repurposing assets: video script, social snippets, Reddit/forum post drafts
- [ ] Update topical map status: pillar/cluster completion tracking
- [ ] Add to AI citation monitoring prompt library

### Velocity and Consolidation Rules

| Rule | Frequency | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Velocity cap | Daily | Max 3–5 posts/day on sites < 12 months old |
| Duplicate detection | Weekly | Merge or redirect overlapping content |
| Thin content audit | Monthly | Expand, consolidate, or remove pages < quality threshold |
| Silo coherence check | Monthly | Ensure clusters link properly; no orphan pages |
| Freshness update | Quarterly | Refresh stats, examples, and schema on top performers |

## 7. Distribution Workflow

Owned content alone is insufficient for AI-era visibility. Experts consistently argue that **off-site mentions, reviews, and third-party corroboration** shape what LLMs recommend.

### Prioritized GEO Pyramid (Ross Simmonds)

Execute in order—do not skip to surface tactics:

```
Tier 1: Owned authoritative pages (indexable, extractable, E-E-A-T rich)
         ↓
Tier 2: Citation outreach on domains LLMs already trust
         ↓
Tier 3: Category language and advanced GEO surface tactics
```

### Channel Strategy

| Channel | Role | Tactics |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Owned blog** | Primary retrieval source for RAG | Bottom-of-funnel pages first; Q&A structure; schema |
| **Reddit** | Long-tail B2B prompt citations (~45% of external citations with YouTube + LinkedIn) | Genuine community participation; answer questions with expertise; link only when additive |
| **YouTube** | High-CPC commercial modifier citations | Turn articles into scripts; publish transcripts; embed on site |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional authority and entity consistency | Executive posts, carousels, document posts; author entity alignment |
| **Review sites** | Corroboration and sentiment | G2, Capterra, Trustpilot—consistent entity descriptions |
| **Industry publications** | PR and citation engineering | Guest posts, data studies, expert quotes |
| **Forums/communities** | Query fan-out coverage | Stack Overflow, niche forums, Discord/Slack communities |

### Surround Sound Strategy (Koray GÜBÜR)

Build a distributed network of mentions confirming entity identity across the web:

- Consistent brand name, product names, and descriptions everywhere
- Positive sentiment in reviews and discussions
- Third-party references that corroborate your claims
- Entity alignment across Wikidata, LinkedIn, Crunchbase, and press

### Repurposing Pipeline (Julian Goldie, Brian Dean)

Every published article triggers an automated or semi-automated repurposing workflow:

```
Published Article
       ↓
├── YouTube/video script
├── Short-form clips (LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram)
├── X/Twitter thread
├── Reddit/community discussion post
├── Newsletter segment
└── PDF/downloadable asset (ungated where possible for citation)
```

**Feedback loop:** Track which hooks and formats perform best; feed winners back into topic selection and content creation.

### Distribution Workflow (Weekly Cadence)

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Monday | Select 2–3 published/updated pages for distribution push |
| Tuesday | Publish LinkedIn + community engagement |
| Wednesday | Release video or audio repurposing asset |
| Thursday | Pursue one citation outreach or PR placement |
| Friday | Review off-site mentions; update entity consistency if needed |

### AI Avatar Guidance (Optional)

Julian Goldie deploys AI avatars for daily short-form on fast-moving topics—with disclosure and human presence reserved for trust-building. **This is not consensus best practice.** Use avatars only for:

- High-cadence, fast-moving AI/tech news
- Short-form social distribution
- Clearly disclosed synthetic presence

Do **not** use avatars for E-E-A-T-critical content, expert guides, or brand authority pages.

## 8. Measurement and KPI Framework

Rankings-only dashboards fail in the AI search era. Measurement must evolve across three connected layers (Aleyda Solis) with RoGEO metrics (Ross Simmonds).

### Layer 1: Presence (Are we visible in AI answers?)

Track whether and how the brand appears across AI platforms.

| KPI | Definition | Target Direction |
|-----|------------|------------------|
| Prompt coverage | % of priority prompts where brand appears | Increase |
| Recommendation rate | % of prompts where brand is actively recommended | Increase |
| Linked citation rate | % of mentions that include a link to owned content | Increase |
| Comparative win rate | % of comparison prompts where brand is favored | Increase |
| Representation accuracy | % of mentions with correct product/description | Increase (aim for >95%) |

**Rules:**

- Segment by platform: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, Google AI Overviews—never blend into one score.
- Use representative prompt libraries reflecting real buyer constraints (price, team size, industry, geography)—not keyword exports.
- Label metrics by confidence: observed, proxy, modeled—never collapse attribution layers.

### Layer 2: Readiness (Why are we visible or invisible?)

When Presence KPIs underperform, diagnose structural causes.

| Readiness Dimension | Diagnostic Signal | Remediation |
|---------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Accessible | Page not indexed or slow | Fix technical SEO |
| Extractable | Mentions without links | Restructure headings, add Q&A blocks, improve schema |
| Differentiated | Generic content, no citations | Add proprietary data, expert input |
| Corroborated | Weak shortlist performance | Invest in distribution and PR |
| Consistent | Misrepresentation in AI answers | Align entity signals across properties |
| Credible | Low E-E-A-T signals | Strengthen author bios, sourcing, trust marks |

**Process:** Map each visibility failure to a readiness dimension before commissioning more content volume.

### Layer 3: Business Impact (Does visibility create value?)

Connect AI presence to commercial outcomes without false precision.

| KPI | Definition |
|-----|------------|
| AI-referred sessions | Traffic from AI platforms (where trackable) |
| AI-influenced pipeline | Leads/sales where AI touchpoint preceded conversion |
| Branded search lift | Increase in brand queries after AI visibility campaigns |
| Content ROI by URL | Performance of pages cited in AI answers vs. those that aren't |

**Rule:** Lead dashboards with the KPI that would most change business outcomes if it moved 20% (Aleyda Solis).

### RoGEO Metrics (Ross Simmonds)

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|------------------|
| Citation frequency | How often your content is cited across AI platforms |
| Reference depth | Whether citations link to specific passages or generic pages |
| Format performance | Which content types (listicles, PDFs, Q&A) get cited most |
| Author authority parsing | Whether AI systems recognize your authors as experts |
| AI-referred lead quality | Conversion rate of AI-influenced traffic vs. other channels |

### Measurement Cadence

| Frequency | Activity |
|-----------|----------|
| Weekly | Spot-check top 20 prompts across 2 platforms; review new citations |
| Monthly | Full Presence audit; readiness scoring on underperforming topics |
| Quarterly | Business impact review; prompt library refresh; dashboard refinement |

### Risk Response Protocol

1. **Detect** — Weekly monitoring of indexing, citation accuracy, and review pass rates.
2. **Triage** — Classify as content quality, technical, distribution, or entity issue using readiness framework.
3. **Remediate** — Rewrite, consolidate, restructure, or distribute—not just "publish more."
4. **Document** — Log incident and update checklist/workflow to prevent recurrence.

## 12. Final Strategic Recommendations

### What the Experts Agree You Should Do

1. **Treat AI as a workflow accelerator, not a strategist.** Humans own strategy, brand fit, and final judgment. AI executes repeatable tasks (Nathan Gotch, Gael Breton, Matt Diggity).

2. **Architect topically before scaling.** Build pillar + cluster silos—or full Semantic Content Networks if you have the capacity—before generating volume (Matt Diggity, Nathan Gotch, Koray GÜBÜR).

3. **Never ship unedited AI.** The cyborg model is universal: AI drafts, humans differentiate. Raw AI publishing is the single highest-risk behavior across all profiles (Lily Ray, Julian Goldie, Matt Diggity).

4. **Optimize for retrieval, not just rankings.** Structure content with question headings, standalone answers, schema, and entity clarity for both Google and LLM citation (Matt Diggity, Ross Simmonds, Aleyda Solis).

5. **Invest in distribution as heavily as production.** Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn, reviews, and PR shape what AI recommends. Owned content alone is insufficient (Ross Simmonds, Mark Williams-Cook, Koray GÜBÜR).

6. **Measure AI presence, readiness, and business impact—not rankings alone.** Build the 3-layer dashboard before scaling content volume (Aleyda Solis, Ross Simmonds).

7. **Apply SEO to defensible businesses.** Generic affiliate content sites are structurally broken for most operators; SEO skills deliver highest ROI on established businesses with real products (Gael Breton).

### Decision Framework for Common Trade-offs

| Question | Recommended Answer | Rationale |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| More volume or better quality? | Quality, within velocity caps | Brian Dean, Matt Diggity, Lily Ray |
| More automation or more review? | Automate research/drafting; never automate final approval | Goldie/Breton with Diggity/Ray gates |
| Keywords or prompts first? | Intent + prompts, validated by keywords | Bridges classic SEO and AI search |
| On-site or off-site first? | On-site authoritative pages, then distribution | Ross Simmonds Prioritized GEO |
| Fundamentals or semantic engineering? | Start fundamentals; add semantic architecture when scaling | Koray GÜBÜR for advanced teams |
| AI avatars or human authors? | Human authors for E-E-A-T content; avatars optional for short-form only | Consensus vs. Goldie exception |

### 90-Day Success Definition

By Day 90, a team running this playbook should have:

- [ ] Documented cyborg workflow with three review gates operational
- [ ] Topical map with at least 2 complete pillar + cluster silos
- [ ] ICP-driven prompt library with grounding scores
- [ ] 24+ published articles passing full readiness checklist
- [ ] Active distribution on 3+ off-site channels
- [ ] 3-layer measurement dashboard reviewed weekly
- [ ] Measurable improvement in at least one Presence KPI
- [ ] Zero raw AI content published
- [ ] Monthly audit cadence established

### The Bottom Line

AI-powered SEO content production is not about generating more pages. It is about building **retrieval-efficient, trust-rich, strategically distributed content systems** where AI handles speed and humans protect authority.

The teams that win in 2026 and beyond will not be those that publish the most AI content. They will be those that combine:

- **Topical architecture** (Diggity, Gotch, Koray)
- **Human-governed quality** (Ray, Dean, Diggity)
- **Agentic workflow speed** (Breton, Goldie)
- **AI-native measurement** (Solis, Simmonds, Williams-Cook)
- **Multi-channel corroboration** (Simmonds, Koray, Williams-Cook)

Start with architecture. Automate the repeatable. Never skip the human. Measure what matters. Distribute everywhere your buyers—and their AI assistants—look for answers.
