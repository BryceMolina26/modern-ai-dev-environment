## Why This Expert Was Selected
Ross Simmonds is the founder of Foundation, a B2B content marketing and SEO agency that has generated 220M+ organic visits for clients including Webex, Mailchimp, Snowflake, and Canva. He is one of the earliest and most systematic voices on Generative Engine Optimization (GEO), publishing frameworks like Prioritized GEO, RoGEO measurement, and the AI Memory Inception strategy. ## Key Ideas Collected

1. **Prioritize owned content before off-site hacks.** Foundation's Prioritized GEO pyramid ranks influence tiers: first, build authoritative pages on your own domain that rank in traditional search (because RAG systems retrieve from indexes); second, pursue citation outreach on domains LLMs already trust; third, invest in category language only after the foundation exists. Most "AI hacks" like key takeaways blocks or `llms.txt` alone don't move citation rates if the underlying content isn't retrievable and justifiable.

2. **Measure RoGEO, not just traffic.** Return on Generative Engine Optimization asks where content is cited across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini; which formats (comparative listicles, opinionated posts, ungated PDFs) actually surface; and whether authors and assets are parsed as authoritative. As AI Mode decouples discovery from clicks, teams misallocate budget when dashboards still reward CTR and backlink volume alone.

3. **Structure content for LLM retrieval and justification.** LLMs need justifiable claims—specific numbers, named experts, dated studies, customer quotes—not generic marketing language. Foundation's GEO stack spans AI alignment (schema, structured author pages, AI-readable sitemaps), information retrieval (HTML, PDFs, YouTube transcripts, Q&A formats with H2-as-prompts), citation engineering (third-party mentions on high-trust domains), and intent alignment (localized, persona-level pain points).

4. **Distribution depth is where B2B brands underinvest.** Foundation's citation research with AirOps found B2B brands own only ~10% of branded citations and ~2% of unbranded ones. Reddit, YouTube, and LinkedIn together account for roughly 45% of external citations. Reddit dominates long-tail B2B prompts; YouTube wins high-CPC commercial modifiers; LinkedIn compounds professional authority. The visibility gap is usually distribution, not production volume.

5. **Build AI memory through a three-layer inception strategy.** Ross frames the next 48 months around LLM personalization and memory: Layer 1 is owned E-E-A-T content; Layer 2 is distribution across channels buyers and models trust; Layer 3 is category creation—owning the language buyers use so models retrieve your framing. Query fan-out means one prompt spawns dozens of sub-queries; brands that plant defensible claims across all three layers shape what gets recommended before buyers ever click.

## Relevance to AI-Powered SEO Content Production

Ross Simmonds treats AI-assisted content production as one pillar inside a broader GEO operating system. His work maps directly to scalable workflows: AI can accelerate drafting and localization, but citation-winning output requires E-E-A-T signals, extractable structure, and multi-channel distribution that LLMs can corroborate. His Prioritized GEO and RoGEO frameworks give content teams a resource-allocation model—when to fan out localized pages, when to invest in Reddit/YouTube/LinkedIn distribution, and how to audit whether AI mentions translate into pipeline—making his perspective essential for teams building AI content engines aimed at AI search visibility, not just organic rankings.

## Practical Takeaways

- Build bottom-of-funnel owned pages first; they feed both Google rankings and RAG retrieval.
- Run citation audits across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini before expanding content volume.
- Structure pages with Q&A blocks, paragraph-level answers, and schema so LLMs can extract and cite.
- Invest in distribution on Reddit, YouTube, and LinkedIn—not just on-site publishing.
- Replace vanity metrics with RoGEO: citation frequency, reference depth, and AI-referred lead quality.
- Use justifiable claims (data, experts, case studies) instead of generic positioning language.
- Pursue category creation: own differentiated terminology, not saturated commodity terms like "best CRM."
- Treat content budgets like asset allocation—study which URLs LLMs already cite in your vertical.
