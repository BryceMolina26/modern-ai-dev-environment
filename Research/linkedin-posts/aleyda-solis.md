## Key Ideas Collected

1. **Expand measurement into three connected layers.** Aleyda's framework separates Presence (whether the brand appears and how it's represented in AI answers), Readiness (whether structural conditions support visibility), and Business Impact (whether visibility creates measurable value). Each layer hands off hypotheses to the next: Presence reveals gaps, Readiness diagnoses structural causes, Business Impact connects to pipeline without overclaiming attribution. Rankings-only dashboards fail because brands can influence purchase decisions inside AI platforms without generating a click.

2. **Build representative prompt libraries, not keyword lists.** Effective AI visibility tracking requires prompts that reflect real buyer constraints—price band, team size, industry, integrations, geography, compliance—not traditional keywords stretched into questions. Prompt libraries should be segmented by market, product line, persona, and journey stage, then aggregated at the topic level because AI outputs vary by session. Presence KPIs include prompt coverage, recommendation rate, linked citation rate, comparative win rate, and representation accuracy.

3. **Diagnose readiness through ten structural characteristics.** When visibility is weak, Aleyda maps gaps to readiness dimensions: Accessible, Extractable, Useful, Fresh, Differentiated, Recognizable, Consistent, Corroborated, Credible, and Transactable. Mentions without links often signal extractability problems; weak shortlist performance often signals corroboration or differentiation gaps; misrepresentation points to entity consistency issues across site, Wikipedia, LinkedIn, and review platforms.

4. **Align every SEO action with brand and topical expertise.** Aleyda's 2026 guidance stresses that informational content must remain meaningful to the brand and connected to product/service offerings—not generic top-of-funnel volume for its own sake. Entity optimization, digital PR, and community management all shape how LLMs describe a brand, since many citations come from external sources like Reddit and industry publications. Schema remains critical for clarifying entities, meaning, and context for LLMs.

5. **Expect blended search experiences, not AI-only SERPs.** She predicts Google will integrate AI Overviews with structured formats like Web Guide rather than replacing classic results with AI Mode as the default. Search is evolving into an AI-integrated landscape where technical foundations (indexability, crawlability, structured data) still feed retrieval systems, but measurement must account for fragmented experiences across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, and Google's AI features.

## Practical Takeaways

- Track AI presence with segmented prompt libraries reflecting real buyer constraints, not keyword exports.
- Lead dashboards with the KPI that would most change business outcomes if it moved 20%.
- Map visibility gaps to readiness characteristics before commissioning more content.
- Ensure entity signals are consistent across site, Wikidata, LinkedIn, reviews, and press.
- Segment measurement by platform—don't blend ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.
- Label metrics by confidence: observed, proxy, and modelled signals should never be blended.
- Ask "Is it on brand?" for every content, technical, and PR action—not just new articles.
- Use schema and structured data as the primary interface between your content and LLM retrieval.
